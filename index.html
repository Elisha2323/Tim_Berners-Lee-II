Tim Berners-Lee, born on June 8, 1955, in London, England, is a computer scientist renowned for inventing the World Wide Web. Educated at The Queen's College, Oxford, and later working at CERN, he proposed the concept of an information management system using hypertext in 1989, leading to the development of the first web browser and server by 1990. His vision of an open, accessible internet revolutionized communication and information sharing globally. Berners-Lee continues to advocate for an open web as a fundamental right and works to ensure its accessibility and neutrality.
